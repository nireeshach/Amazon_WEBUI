November 30, 2012
I was not sure if I should get the Bowflex SelectTech 552 Dumbbells or the Universal Power Pak. The Bowflex SelectTech 552 don't seem to be very durable. There are YouTube videos showing the plastic selector plates break easy and they show how to repair with glue since they are breaking so frequently. They also talk about ordering other parts on EBay for repairs, so apparently there are issues with other parts. They also say you need to set the dumbbells down gently in order not to break the plastic selector plates. I needed dumbbells that are durable. Sometimes I can't set dumbbells down gently due to I am exhausted after the set. You also have to set/adjust the weight on BOTH sides of each dumbbell. I thought the Bowflex may be nice since you can adjust in 2.5 lb. increments up to 25 lbs. But then I started thinking that I have never been able to adjust conventional type dumbbells in 2.5 lb. increments, so what's the point.
Based on the aforementioned, I purchased the Universal Power Pak. The Universals have been great. You can set/adjust the weight on either side of each dumbbell, which is very convenient. They come with a stand, but you don't need to use the stand if you want to save space. You only need the trays the dumbbell rests in. The trays can sit on the floor, table, or mounted to the stand. I saw other reviews where some said they don't like the stand design because it doesn't use a pedestal. The stand design is great because it DOESN'T use a pedestal. The stand design allows you to more easily store stuff under it. Some other reviewer complained about the weight plates being noisy clanking together. I do not see this as a problem at all. They are quieter than the old school steel weight plates on a dumbbell hand bars I had been using. I hope this review helps others. It was very difficult for me to decide between the two types. I have been very happy with this purchase.

allinav
5.0 out of 5 starsAfter a Lot of Research, I Picked These...
November 14, 2014
Color: grayVerified Purchase
I'm absolutely thrilled with these weights and the stand. After a lot of research, I narrowed my choices for a set of adjustable weights down to three. These, the Bowflex select-tech weights and the reebok powerblocks.

I was able to knock the Reebok Powerblocks off my list and narrow it down to just the Bowflex and these because I realized I needed/wanted a "knob" to change the weights. I watched a few videos that demonstrated how you change the weight for the Reebok Powerblocks and knew right away it wasn't for me. There is too much room for error, it's not as quick as a knob, when exhausted I could imagine it being a real PIA...in other words, I wanted something easier & more reliable with no room for error.

I ended up going with these over the Bowflex SelectTech weights because of one thing and one thing only...the included stand. Let me tell you right now, you may not think a stand is a big deal, but it absolutely is...especially with an adjustable dumbbell. You aren't just tossing the weights onto a rack like you do with regular dumbbells, you have to "line them up" when putting them back (which is the case with ANY adjustable dumbbell). That means a certain amount of control is required, and with the weights up off the floor on a stand, it makes it infinitely easier to more quickly & easily put them back, change the weight and pick them back up.

These weights are roughly $30 cheaper than the Bowflex weights, and they come with a stand. When you consider the fact that the Bowflex weight stand is an additional $120 or so, that essentially makes these weights $150 cheaper when you really compare "apples to apples".

Granted, these weights only go up to 45, and the Bowflex weights go up to 52.5, but I could always just put on some 5lb wrist straps to make up the difference. I wouldn't consider that worth the extra $150.

Also, these weights are only adjustable in 5lb increments, the Bowflex weights are adjustable by 2.5lbs (up until you get to 25lbs I think, then you can only adjust those in 5lb increments as well). Again however, not something I consider a negative. I've never adjusted my weights by 2.5lb increments in my life. If you're a woman and you want that kind of fine tuning ability, that may be a bigger deal.

I was worried about some of the other reviewers mentioning that the plates "rattle"...I honestly am just convinced there is no pleasing some people. The plates rattle by MILLIMETERS...it's not like the weights are sliding all over the place. I've been using these for skull crushers and other moves where the weight is above my face/head/feet and am supremely confident in them. They are stable & secure, and there is nothing that has made me question that. They look and feel extremely well made and of a great quality.

Something I didn't expect, but am extremely happy about, is that no matter what weight you select, the amount of weight on each side of the dumbbell is the same. Some of the really cheap $50 to $100 adjustable dumbbells that only go up to 20-25lbs are adjustable in 5lb increments, but when you set it on, say, 15lbs, you'll end up with 10lbs on one side of the dumbbell, and 5lbs on the other. So while you are in fact lifting 15lbs, you have to deal with the issue of having an uneven weight. These are not like that and you get exactly the same amount of weight on both sides of the dumbbell regardless of your weight selection.

I've been using these with the BeachBody "Body Beast" program. I've done P90X in the past, and I can tell you that you need far more available weight options AND the ability to change them FASTER than you do in P90X. In other words, I needed a set of adjustable dumbbells with a lot of weight options, the ability to quickly change weights and the ability to change the weight reliably even when exhausted. These are perfect for the "Body Beast" program, which means they would be even better for use with P90X, as the requirements aren't as strict in comparison.

These weights deserve all five of the stars I've given them, really really happy.

5.0 out of 5 starsBetter than back buddy for the back, pair it up with a foam roller for your mobility needs
February 9, 2016
Color: New VersionVerified Purchase
I don't have other types of lacrosse balls but I still want to add my personal experience to these great mobility tools since I can compare it to my other mobility devices. So I have the back buddy, which is a theracane like self massager, and a giant foam roller. In my indulgence of physical fitness they have all been instrumental in being able to continue to train. However, in my experience, these balls almost completely replace the back buddy. I have more control both in terms of location and pressure when compared to cane. Second, because of the give, I'm not as worried applying too much pressure to cause bruising. Lastly because of the tackiness of the silicone material, and due to the spherical shape where it rolls rather than slides, the design reduces skin discomfort compared to the cane. The smaller footprint and ease of cleaning is just a bonus. Compared to the foam roller, it provides a more focused application of pressure. The blunt shape of the foam roller is great for the leg muscles like calves, hamstrings, glutes and quads, but the ball is more useful around the back and on the shins. To not completely discount the cane, I will say it has some use in the upper neck area.

Bottom line: If you need to cover most of your mobility needs, start with these balls and some type of large blunt foam roller.

4.0 out of 5 starsReally Glad I Chose These
August 2, 2013
Color: grayVerified Purchase
I'm not completely sure I shouldn't give this 5 stars, but for my personal order I'm going to give it 4 stars. I'll start with the negatives to justify:

- one of the weights doesn't click into the stand perfectly when I replace it.  It needs to click in so you can change weights. However, the other one is perfect so I'm sure this is a hit or miss issue and most people won't experience it. 

- the weights are some sort of formed plastic and they warn you against dropping them. I'm not a weight dropper anyway, but if the product was a stronger material I don't think I'd feel the need to be so careful when exercises position them near the ground. 

That's about it for negatives, and that's not bad. Now for positives:

- I'm doing P90X and the ease of increasing or decreasing weight is MUCH better for my workouts than the first two rounds I did (where I had about 3 fixed weights to work with for each workout...5 lbs, 12 lbs, 30-40 lbs not-quickly-adjustable weight, so I had to stick with one choice the entire workout)

- It's so easy to turn the dial to adjust weights, and the amount of weight is clearly labeled

- Solid stand included that doesn't take up too much space for my taste, and I don't have to worry about it falling over

- Attractive looking weights, like I'm working out in that future time where everyone is wearing silver clothes all the time

- The weights do make some noise as you lift when doing anything jerky, but I have zero concern they will come off at this point 

- I was worried the weights would be too long, like the Bowflex, but I've now done every P90X exercise in the first 3 weeks and haven't had an issue.  (if I can, I'll make a video showing me doing each exercise with these)

All in all, at this point I have no regrets about buying these. Please do keep in mind they come in one 122 lb box. I had to carry them 3 stories up 6 sets of stairs, and that was my first intense workout with them. Maybe open the box and take the stand up first, then the weights second and possibly third. Otherwise carry the whole box like I did (IF you are already in good shape) and feel like you're in a Strongman competition.

4.0 out of 5 starsExcellent Dumbbells, just know the limitations
March 21, 2015
Color: grayVerified Purchase
These dumbbells have held up well and I am quite pleased with my purchase. I have had this set two plus years and bought on a lighting sale at less than half retail price. The set arrived well packaged. The stand took a short time to assemble and is very useful. The dumbbells each sit on a base which securely holds the individual weight plates, and each base can then be attached to the stand or just left on the floor if you prefer not to use the stand. I purchased this Universal set because I did not want at least five or more sets of dumbbells, and I reasoned that the limited exercises for which I planned to use this set would tend not to put much stress on the set. I use these dumbbells primarily for standing curls and less often, triceps extensions. Yes, the dumbbells are big but you quickly get used to it and the movement is not materially affected. The weight selection dial is quick and always puts the right amount of weight on the bar. When you pick the dumbbell up from the stand, the unneeded weights are left behind securely sitting on the base. The plates on the dumbbell do rattle and the thought will cross your mind that perhaps a plate could fall off in mid-exercise, but this has never happen and given that I do no exercises where I could get hit in the face or head by a flying plate, it is not a concern for me.
I do treat these dumbbells carefully, never dropping them on the floor and almost always placing them directly back onto the base on the stand. I do not think these dumbbells would last long if they were not so treated. That treatment, however, is perhaps also the negative, as I find myself thinking towards the last few hard reps that I need to have energy and focus to carefully place the dumbbells back on the base (you have to maneuver the dumbbell back onto the base because the plates left behind must slide back into the open slots of the dumbbell). If you tend to push yourself to the limit for those last few reps and drop the dumbbell onto the floor from exhaustion, or you do exercises like fly extensions where you want the two dumbbells to touch, or the rattle of the plates on the dumbbells would distract you, then this Universal set is probably not for you.

ShopperInColorado
5.0 out of 5 starsGreat for the price!
June 5, 2014
Color: grayVerified Purchase
These weights are a perfect addition for those who work out at home!

I'm currently doing a DVD program that requires constant switching of weights and these are perfect for fast changes without having the hassle of having 10 sets of weights floating around the living room, not to mention the expense of buying such a set that can go up to 50 pounds!

They are very fast to switch weight and I haven't noticed having to wiggle the weights in their stand to get it to switch as some reviewers mentioned. The handle is also soft and comfortable and is made of a material that won't get slippery when your hands are sweaty (as some of my stand alone dumbbells made of metal did).

A couple of things that could be seen as bothersome: The stand was difficult to put together as a couple of the bolts weren't cut correctly and I had to have my husband help me crank them in (which is why I'm lifting weights, I have the strength of a willow tree - but seriously, even he struggled). The stand is also a bit unsightly - it's currently in my living room but I'm thinking of moving it somewhere else because I don't want to stare at the thing when I'm watching TV. It's too tall to hide under my side tables (which is where I kept my old dumbbell set(s), and sort of leans forward a bit so it's difficult to put behind furniture or in nooks or crannys in your work out space. But it came with a stand whereas other manufacturers charge another $100 for that option. And it is great not having to squat or bend down to switch the weights, which comes in handy at higher weight settings.

Also, the weights are heavier on the side that faces up when they are in their stand. It's more comfortable to hold them with the heavier side facing down especially when doing shoulder work (as makes sense from a gravitational perspective) but then you have to switch your grip to get them into their holder the proper way. This could be difficult if you are at a higher weight setting and even only using 15 pounds this morning, I almost dropped one trying to rotate it to fit in the stand the proper way. I don't know why the weight isn't evenly distributed. That's honestly my biggest complaint, but again, you just have to be careful and take your time when lifting heavy stuff.

They also do clank together when lifting them around. It's jarring at first, especially if you haven't been using plated dumbbells, but you get used to it. It isn't loud, but again, if you aren't expecting it, it kind of gives you a jolt the first few times it happens.

Overall, they seem very sturdy and I'm not worried at all about safety. So for the price, they seem really solid and I give them 5 stars. I'm very happy with my purchase.
